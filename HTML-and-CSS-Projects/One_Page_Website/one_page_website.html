Introduction

Knowing how to change a tire is an imperative skill for all drivers. While most learn how to do so in their childhood many don't retin the knowledge into their adulthood. This webpage will provide you, the reader, with the skills necessary for changing a tire. Don't worry, if videos are an easier method to learn I have provided one at the end of this article.

Tools

In order to change a tire you will need tire caulks, jack, a tire iron, and a spare tire.

How To Change a Tire

  1. Find a Safe Location-


    You'll want to find a level location that is safe from debris and hazards. If you have experienced a flat tire while driving calmly pull over, come to a complete stop, and turn on your hazards.

  2. Appy Wheel Wedges and Remove Hubcap or Wheel Covers-


    Place wheel wedges infornt or behind tires. If your flat is in the front place them behind your rear tires. If the flat is in the back put them infornt of the front tires. This will ensure that the vehicle does not roll while working on it. If applicable remove the hubcap or wheel covers per your owner manuals instructions.

  3. Loosen the Lug Nuts-


    Using the tire iron turn the lug nuts counterclockwise until you break the nuts loose. DO NOT REMOVE THEM. Loosen them only 1/2 or 1 full turn.

  4. Place the Jack and Raise the Vehicle-


    Place the jack under the vehicle. Your owns manual will indicate where is the best location for your specific vehicle for it to be placed. Ensure that the jack is on flat and solid ground then begin to raise the jack until the vehicle tire is 3-6in off the ground.

  5. Unscrew the Lug Nuts and Remove the Tire-


    Using the tire iron unscrew all the lug nuts now securing the flat tire, set them aside in a safe location where they won't be lost, and remove the tire.

  6. Mount the Spare Tire and Tighten Them By Hand-


    You'll want to mount the tire on the lug bolts, push the tire firmly back, and hand tighten the nuts. You'll tighten them fully once the vehicle is lowered.

  7. Lower The Vehicle and Tighthen the Lug Nuts-


    Lower the vehicle until it is resting on the ground but not fully to were the tire is bearing the weight of the vehicle. Using the tire iron tighten the nuts as much as possible.

  8. Lower the Vehile Fully, Remove the Jack, and Tighten the Nuts-


    Lower the vehicle fully until the jack is no longer supporting the weight of the car. Remove the jack stand and firmly apply enough pressure to each lug nut to ensure it is fully tightened.

  9. Take you Flat Tire to a Techincian


    At this point you are safe to drive. It is advised that you take your car and flat tire to a technician so that they may repair it and reinstall.
